The origin story

Why I built this

Working in growth at Trupeer.ai, a big part of my job is making sure people can find us — through search, through content, through word of mouth. I was deep into SEO, content marketing, and product tutorials when I started noticing a shift.

More and more of our users were saying they first heard about Trupeer from ChatGPT or Gemini. Not Google. Not a blog post. An AI chat. But when I tried to figure out how often we were being recommended, how we compared to competitors, or which queries triggered our citations — there was no tool for that.

I'd ask AI chatbots myself, manually, one query at a time. It was slow, inconsistent, and impossible to scale. So I built AI Citation Tracker to solve exactly that problem — for myself first, and then for everyone doing growth and SEO work in the age of AI.

"More users were saying they found us through ChatGPT. But there was no tool to track that. So I built one."

What I believe in

Values that drive the work

01

Build for real problems

AI Citation Tracker exists because I needed it. The best tools come from genuine frustration — not feature lists or market maps. If I'm annoyed enough to build it, others probably need it too.

02

Make it free to start

Growth tools should be accessible. That's why Gemini integration is free — because not every marketer has a budget for yet another paid tool. Start free, upgrade when you need to.

03

Privacy first, always

Your API keys, your domains, your data — it stays in your browser. No accounts, no databases, no analytics. I wouldn't want my tools tracking me, so this one doesn't track you.

04

Keep shipping

v2.0 → v2.1 → v2.2 → v2.3 → v2.4. Each version shipped because users asked for something real. The roadmap is driven by what people actually need, not by what looks impressive.
